About VanaRaksha

An IoT-powered smart plant intelligence system honouring India's botanical heritage through real-time sensor monitoring and data analytics.

🌱 Project Overview

VanaRaksha (वन रक्षा — "Guardian of the Forest") is an academic IoT project that combines hardware sensor simulation, real-time data visualisation, and botanical knowledge into a single unified dashboard.

The system monitors 10 culturally significant Indian plants across 6 environmental parameters, generating live alerts, health scores, and care recommendations — all in a browser-based dashboard requiring zero installation.

"Technology as a bridge between ancient botanical wisdom and modern data intelligence."

🔬 Monitored Parameters

  • 💧 Soil Moisture (%) — critical for plant hydration and root health
  • 🌡️ Temperature (°C) — ambient and root-zone thermal monitoring
  • 🌫️ Air Humidity (%) — leaf transpiration and disease prevention
  • ☀️ Light (Lux) — photosynthesis rate and growth optimisation
  • ⚗️ Soil pH — nutrient availability and root uptake efficiency
  • 🪣 Water Level (%) — irrigation tank and reservoir monitoring

🏛️ Design Philosophy

The visual identity draws from India's natural colour palette — deep forest greens, warm creams, and golden accents — combined with Cormorant Garamond (an elegant serif) for botanical authenticity. Mandala-inspired rotating rings in the background pay homage to India's geometric art traditions.

Every design decision balances cultural resonance with modern usability — making the dashboard feel like a premium product while remaining accessible and performant on any device.

🌿 Plant Selection Rationale

The 10 plants were selected to represent a breadth of Indian flora — from sacred Vedic plants (Tulsi, Lotus) to culinary staples (Curry Leaf), medicinal giants (Neem, Aloe Vera), ornamentals (Rose, Mogra), and modern indoor favourites (Snake Plant, Money Plant, Spider Plant). Each has distinct environmental requirements, making them ideal for demonstrating diverse sensor profiles.
